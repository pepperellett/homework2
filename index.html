About Me

Originally from Dallas, Texas, I grew up studying fine art which eventually lead me to attend the School of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston. It took me about two years of working in a contemporary gallery before I got completely fed up with the hypocrisy of the art world.

After I left, I sought out working in the jewelry industry because I wanted to spend my time and skillset supporting women who make things with their hands, which at the time, felt like the antithesis of the art world. Within a few years, I had built out all production and operations systems for a Brooklyn-based jewelry company called Catbird and that's how I discovered my love for building systems and being able to connect all of the seemingly disparate moving parts in a given structure.

I now work for a smaller jewelry designer as a production and operations director, building her first inventory and production system from the ground up. Outside of jewelry production, I'm committed to seeing a public safety app I designed come to fruition and it's both of these parts of my life that have motivated me to enroll in this boot camp.
